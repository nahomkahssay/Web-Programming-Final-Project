Tej

Tej is a mead or honey wine that is brewed and consumed in Ethiopia and Eritrea. It is flavored with the powdered leaves and twigs of gesho (Rhamnus prinoides), a hops-like bittering agent that is a species of buckthorn.

Tej is usually homemade, but throughout Ethiopia it is available in "tej houses" (tej betoch, singular tej bet (ጠጅ ቤት)).

Ingredients

  • 21 ounces (621 ml) liquid honey
  • 63 ounces (1.86 L) water
  • ½ teaspoon (1.6 g) brewer’s yeast
  • 5 ounces (142 g) gesho

The Culture of drinking Tej

Tej forms an important part of Ethiopian society and culture and is considered the national drink of Ethiopia. In Ethiopia, Tej is often homemade or served at Tej houses, and is often served in a flask-like pitcher or bottle, called a berele.

A different beverage, berz, is Ethiopian honey water.
